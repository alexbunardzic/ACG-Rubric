# Inter-Assessor Agreement

*(Structured stub.)*

## Why we measure it

A rubric that produces different scores from different assessors on the same evidence is not a rubric; it is a mood. This document records how we measure whether the ACG Rubric is producing consistent outcomes across assessors.

## Method

*(To be drafted. Sketch:)*

- **Paired assessment.** Two assessors independently assess the same subject, from the same evidence, without communication until both worksheets are complete.
- **Comparison.** Per-criterion scores are compared. Disagreements are catalogued by criterion, not aggregated into a single number.
- **Root cause.** For each disagreement: was the criterion under-specified? Was the evidence ambiguous? Did the assessors interpret the same word differently?

## What we do with disagreement

Disagreements are treated as defects in the rubric, not defects in the assessors. Each is filed against the relevant criterion. The pattern of disagreements over time drives clarifying revisions to rubric text.
